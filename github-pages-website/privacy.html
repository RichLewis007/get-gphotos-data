Privacy Policy

get-gphotos-data Application

Last Updated: January 2025

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how the get-gphotos-data application (the "Application") collects, uses, and protects your information. This policy complies with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for users in the European Union and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and other applicable US privacy laws.

By using this Application, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Authentication Data

The Application uses Google OAuth 2.0 for authentication. The following data is stored locally on your device:

  • OAuth Tokens: Access tokens and refresh tokens provided by Google are stored locally on your device in the application data directory.
  • Credentials: Your OAuth client credentials file is stored locally (you provide this file; it is not collected by us).

2.2 Google Photos Data

When you use the Application, it accesses the following data from your Google Photos account:

  • Media items (photos and videos) uploaded by the Application
  • Albums created by the Application
  • Metadata associated with media items (creation dates, dimensions, camera settings, etc.)
  • Album information (titles, item counts, sharing status)

Important: The Application only accesses media items and albums that were created by the Application itself. It does not access your existing Google Photos library.

2.3 Application Usage Data

The Application may generate local log files on your device for error tracking and debugging purposes. These logs may contain:

  • Error messages and stack traces
  • API request/response information
  • Application state information

All log files are stored locally on your device and are never transmitted to external servers.

3. How We Use Your Information

The Application uses the collected information for the following purposes:

  • Authentication: OAuth tokens are used to authenticate API requests to Google Photos
  • Data Display: Google Photos data is retrieved and displayed in the Application's user interface
  • Error Handling: Local logs are used for debugging and improving the Application

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Local Storage

All data is stored locally on your device:

  • OAuth Tokens: Stored in your system's application data directory
    • macOS: ~/Library/Application Support/get-gphotos-data/
    • Windows: %APPDATA%\get-gphotos-data\
    • Linux: ~/.config/get-gphotos-data/
  • Log Files: Stored in the same application data directory
  • Application Settings: Stored using your operating system's standard settings storage

4.2 Data Transmission

The Application communicates directly with Google's servers via the Google Photos Library API. No data is transmitted to third-party servers or to the Application's developers.

4.3 Security Measures

  • OAuth tokens are stored securely using your operating system's standard security mechanisms
  • All API communications use HTTPS encryption (enforced by Google's API)
  • The Application does not store your Google account password or credentials file contents
  • OAuth refresh tokens are used to automatically renew access tokens when they expire

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

The Application does not share your data with any third parties.

  • No data is sent to external servers (except Google's API servers for authentication and data retrieval)
  • No analytics or tracking services are used
  • No data is sold, rented, or disclosed to third parties
  • The Application is open-source software, allowing you to review the code to verify these practices

6. Your Rights (GDPR and CCPA)

6.1 Right to Access

You have the right to access all data stored by the Application. Since all data is stored locally on your device, you can access it directly through your file system.

6.2 Right to Deletion

You can delete your data at any time by:

  • Deleting the application data directory (this removes all stored tokens and logs)
  • Uninstalling the Application
  • Revoking access through your Google Account settings at myaccount.google.com/permissions

6.3 Right to Data Portability

All data stored by the Application is in standard formats (JSON) and is accessible on your local device. You can copy, export, or move this data as needed.

6.4 Right to Object and Restriction

You can stop using the Application at any time, delete stored data, and revoke Google Photos API access through your Google Account settings.

6.5 California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information is collected
  • Know whether your personal information is sold or disclosed
  • Opt-out of the sale of personal information (this Application does not sell data)
  • Access your personal information
  • Request deletion of your personal information
  • Non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights

7. Children's Privacy

The Application is not intended for use by children under the age of 13 (or 16 in the EU). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact us immediately so we can delete the information.

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes.

9. Third-Party Services

The Application integrates with Google Photos Library API. Your use of Google services is subject to Google's Privacy Policy, which can be found at policies.google.com/privacy.

11. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

Under GDPR, our legal basis for processing your personal data is:

  • Consent: You provide explicit consent when you authenticate with Google and grant permissions to access Google Photos data
  • Legitimate Interest: Processing is necessary for the functionality of the Application as requested by you

12. Data Retention

Data is retained on your device until you:

  • Delete the application data directory
  • Uninstall the Application
  • Revoke access through Google Account settings

OAuth tokens may be automatically refreshed by Google's authentication system, but you can revoke access at any time through your Google Account settings.

13. International Data Transfers

All data is stored locally on your device. When the Application communicates with Google's servers, data transfers are subject to Google's data transfer mechanisms and privacy policies. Google's services comply with applicable data protection laws, including GDPR.
